Camp Zoo at the Long Island Game Farm

The Long Island Game Farm is inviting children from all over Long Island to Camp Zoo. Camp Zoo is a weeklong day camp with weekly sessions running through July and August.

Designed for children 5 - 15 years of age, Camp Zoo will provide children with a fun-filled, rich learning environment they will remember all year.

Each day at Camp Zoo will offer new and exciting experiences such as zoo-tours, close-up animal encounters, NS nature walks. In addition, there will be arts and crafts activities, animal stories and sing-alongs, nature-related games, and informative animal tours. Special behind the scenes activities will be offered that are only available to children who attend Camp Zoo.

Camp Zoo has been developed with the guidance of Irene Nowell, former Superintendent of Schools at Remsenburg-Speonk UFSD and current Education Field Supervisor at Dowling College.

This year Holly Coyle has accepted our offer to run the camp and brings with her terrific combination of animal science, ecology and wilderness training as well as experience as a camp teacher and curiculum planner. Holly will have a camp assistant working with her to give eachchild as much attention as possible. She is planning nature hikes, scavenger hunts and lots of up close and personal experiences with our animals.

Each session will take place at the Game Farm from 10:00 a.m. to 3:00 p.m., Monday through Friday. Camp is held rain or shine. Campers will receive a simple mid-morning and afternoon snack. Campers will provide their their own lunch and may purchase juice/soda. Cups of water will be provided.

Registration for Camp Zoo begins on April 1, 2005. Cost per weekly session is $294.00, tax included. The Long Island Game Farm can accept about 15 campers for each session of Camp Zoo. Considering the limited class sizes, Camp Zoo is expected to fill up quickly, so sign up early.

To register, or for more information about Camp Zoo, e-mail campzoo@longislandgamefarm.com or call (631) 878-6670.
