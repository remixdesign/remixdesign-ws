Regional Winners of FIRST Robotics Competition Receive Recognition for Outstanding Achievement

On March 18 and 19, over 10,000 spectators gathered at Hofstra Arena, Hofstra University in Hempstead to watch 40 teams of high school students compete at the Long Island Regional competition to earn a spot at the FIRST USA Championship to be held April 21-23 at the Georgia Dome in Atlanta GA. Several teams received honors and recognition that rewarded design excellence, competitive play, sportsmanship and high-impact partnerships between schools, businesses and communities.

Read the full release

Dubbed the “NCAA of Smarts,” the annual competition focuses on a no-holds-barred struggle among 150 pound robots designed and built by joint teams of high school students and corporate engineers. While fierce competition is the order of the day, winning is within very controlled game rules with point scores determining the ultimate victors.

FIRST (For Inspiration and Recognition of Science and Technology) is a nation-wide program, founded in 1989 by inventor and entrepreneur, Dean Kamen, with an imperative, singular focus of exciting more young people about the fun, accessibility, and importance of science and engineering.
