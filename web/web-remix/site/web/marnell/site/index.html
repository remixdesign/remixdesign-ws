A recent article in the "Family Matters" section of the Wall Street Journal presents a growing trend towards controlled separations. Instead of seeking a legal separation with the counsel of a lawyer, a couple may plan with a marriage counselor to live apart for a limited period of time, communicating with each other only within predetermined guidelines. To this end, to couple intends to slow down or attempt to stop the building uncertainty and slow emotional descent that typically plagues troubled marriages.

A recent article in New York Law Journal reports on several recent civil rulings where domestic partners were denied certain rights and benefits typically granted to married couples. Though the cases have not ruled in favor of the partners' rights, they represent attempts to re-examine what rights of married partners can be legally applied to unmarried partners.
